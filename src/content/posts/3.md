Meetings are expensive. For fixed-scope builds, we default to async and reserve calls for decisions only. Here’s the playbook.

## Core rituals
- **Daily loom recap** — 3–5 minutes covering progress, blockers, and next steps.
- **Written decisions** — we use short decision docs with context, options, and the chosen path.
- **Two feedback lanes** — “blocking” (must change) and “polish” (nice-to-have) to keep momentum.

## Tooling stack
- **Project board:** three columns—To Build, Review, Done.
- **Docs:** one shared brief, one changelog, one QA checklist.
- **Assets:** single folder with locked filenames to avoid version hell.

## Cadence that sticks
- Monday/Wednesday/Friday updates for stakeholders.
- Tuesday/Thursday internal QA cycles with a 20-item checklist.
- Friday handoff notes: what shipped, what’s next, how to request changes.

When everyone knows the rules of engagement, we get faster builds, cleaner feedback, and fewer surprises.
